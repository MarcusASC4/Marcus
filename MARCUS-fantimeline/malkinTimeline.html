Evgeni Malkin Timeline:

2004:

In 2005, Evgeni Malkin was drafted in the first round by the struggling Pittsburgh Penguins franchise. He accomplished his life-long dream of being a part of this historic franchise.

2006:

Prior to joining the Penguins, Malkin was widely considered the best player in the world not in the NHL. He is an excellent passer and playmaker and made several standout plays in the exhibition game before getting hurt. This was the year when Gino made his NHL debut, and started a Hall of Fame level career, immediatley winning the Rookie of the Year award.

2008:

In only his second year in the National Hockey League, Malkin was the runner-up in MVP voting and was an all-star for the second year in a row. He has completely solidified his status as one of the best players in the world.

2009:

This was the defining year for the Pittsburgh Penguins franchise, Gino led this team to their first Stanley Cup title since 1992. He led the NHL in points and once again came in second place in MVP voting. As a result of his overwhelming dominance on the ice, Malkin won the playoff MVP award for the first time in his career.
